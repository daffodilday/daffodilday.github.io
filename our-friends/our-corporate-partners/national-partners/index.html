The success of Daffodil Day comes from the generosity of all Australians with their time and money, including the corporate community. Here we take the time to acknowledge our fabulous corporate partners who support Cancer Council’s Daffodil Day. With their help we can continue to fund research, provide support services and give hope to the one in two people who are diagnosed with cancer in their lifetime.

Read more about how our corporate partners support us nationally, and in NSW, NT, SA, VIC, QLD and WA.

Coles Supermarkets and Coles Express are proud to be Daffodil Day’s biggest supporters – having raised over $19.5 million for the cause to date, with over $2.8 million raised in 2014 alone. All funds raised by Coles Supermarkets and Coles Express go towards Cancer Council support services in your state or territory.

2015 marks the 20th anniversary of Coles Supermarkets’ support of Daffodil Day. Show your support this year at Coles by picking up some Daffodil Day merchandise, including exclusive Peppa Pig stickers and reusable shopping bags, fresh or potted daffodils or make a donation in store.

www.coles.com.au

Coles Express is a proud partner of Cancer Council’s Daffodil Day in 2015. Stores will be raising funds through a range of Cancer Council merchandise featuring Peppa Pig, collection tins and in conjunction with Shell, the donation of 2c for every litre of fuel sold on Daffodil Day. Money raised will help fund cancer support services.

Coles Express is a partner of Cancer Council’s Daffodil Day for the fifth consecutive year. In 2014 alone, Coles Express and Shell raised over $1M to help fund cancer support services.

Visit your local Coles Express store to support a great cause!

www.colesexpress.com.au

Gordon and Gotch is proud to be in its seventh year of supporting Daffodil Day and Cancer Council. 
As Australia’s oldest and largest independent magazine distributor, Gordon and Gotch supplies 130 million copies annually to over 8,500 retail outlets including newsagents, convenience stores and supermarkets. 
Employing over 150 people with operations in Australia and New Zealand, the organisation represents over 450 global publishers with nearly 3,000 magazine titles in all interest categories. 
The staff and management of Gordon and Gotch are excited to once again join the fight against cancer.


www.gordongotch.com.au

StarTrack is proud to be a long-standing supporter of Daffodil Day.

Each year, StarTrack transports Daffodil Day merchandise boxes nation-wide between June and August, which involves almost 18,000 individual deliveries*.

Like Cancer Council, StarTrack is committed to service excellence. StarTrack services both business and consumer markets with an unrivalled range of parcel, freight, express, courier and international solutions under one banner. All of our services are developed with customers' needs in mind – choice, convenience, reliability, flexibility and control.

StarTrack's support helps fund cancer research into new and better treatments, education and support services that make a difference in our community.

*Daffodil Day 2012

www.startrack.com.au

Rockmans first committed support to Daffodil Day in 1999 and are delighted to continue helping the local community by supporting the fundraising efforts of Cancer Council. Today, Rockmans has over 280 retail outlets across Australia and the brand delivers Australian women on trend, quality fashion pieces at unexpected prices. With new styles arriving in store each week, you will be sure to find fashion that will fit, flatter and elevate your style. 

 

www.rockmans.com.au

Each garment is designed in silhouettes perfect for a curvy figure to create flattering styles and boost inner confidence. beme is available in 25 stand alone stores nationally and over 140 Rockmans stores, be beautifully styled from size 14 – 26. 

www.beme.com.au

As Australia’s largest fabric, craft and home decorator superstore, Spotlight is proud to be supporting one of the best-known and most popular fundraising events in Australia, Cancer Council’s Daffodil Day.

Spotlight is Australian-owned and operated and has developed a business philosophy and culture centred on people who make a difference, which is why Spotlight is dedicated to the fight against cancer. Visit one of Spotlight’s 150 store locations to purchase Daffodil Day product or to make a donation.

www.spotlight.com.au

At Anaconda, Australia’s largest camping, adventure and sports superstore, we are invested in developing stronger and healthier communities and are proud to support Cancer Council’s Daffodil Day. Daffodil Day merchandise is available for sale and donations can be made at our 30+ stores Australia-wide.

www.anaconda.com.au

As Australia’s largest not-for-profit health fund, HCF is proud to be supporting one of the best-known and most popular fundraising events in Australia, Cancer Council’s Daffodil Day.

We’re passionate about the health and wellbeing of our members which is why we are dedicated to showing we care this Daffodil Day.  Visit one of our 50 locations around Australia to purchase a pin or make a donation.
